# v0.9.22 — Fingertip: automated where possible, one click where not

Wave 13, built to the operator's brief: make life easy.

## The live pane respects you

* Opened a row's details mid-run? The pane **pauses its updates while you
  read** ("paused while reading — close to resume") and applies everything
  queued the moment you close it. No more content yanked from under you,
  no more clicks lost to a repaint.
* A small **spinner appears beside "Live"** whenever anything is running
  in the background — jobs, executions — so you always know work is
  happening without hunting for it.

## Dependencies, handled

* The packages tools and forged tools most often need — **pandas,
  beautifulsoup4, lxml, geopy, python-dateutil** — now ship with
  `pip install systemu`. The most common approval interruption is gone.
* Anything still needing approval becomes a **one-click card in
  Needs-you** — including from quick chat tasks, which previously just
  errored. The error itself now tells you where the button is.

## Recording, smoother

* **Stop & Analyze** now says exactly what happens next and takes you to
  Work, where the new workflow appears — Needs-you rings the moment it's
  ready to review.
* Finished workflows get a **Run again** button: the repeatability payoff,
  one click.

## Honest verdicts

A quick answer that admits "could not complete" now reports **partial**,
never success — the model renders its own honest verdict on every answer.

## Quiet reliability

* A daemon that loses the port race now **refuses to start** with a clear
  message instead of running headless into the wrong vault.
* Docker installs that fall back to schema-create now **stamp the
  migration baseline**, so future upgrades apply cleanly.
* The README is rewritten as a product narrative (the teach-by-doing moat,
  the governance story, a quick start that matches the real first-run),
  and the public repository dropped its dead lockfiles and stale
  changelog.

## Named slips (next wave)

Inbox (park/resume) were planned for this wave and deliberately held to
keep the release tight — they lead the next one.
