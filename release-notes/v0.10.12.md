# v0.10.12 — Put things on the table yourself (R-B2 / T2b-1)

Curation (v0.10.11) let you tidy what systemu already saw. Now you can **declare what it can't see yet** —
a service you use, a folder, a credential you keep by name — and it stays on your table until the real
thing shows up and takes its place.

## What ships

- **"+ Put on the table"** — a palette in the page header (and on the empty-state) to declare a
  **service / account**, a **folder**, or a **credential by name**. Declared items land as *declared*
  (intent, not yet configured) and are yours — an operator-added item can only ever be created by you,
  directly, in this UI.
- **They survive.** A purely-declared item used to be silently wiped by the 60-second reconcile (which
  rebuilds the board from what's actually connected). Now declarations live in their own store that the
  reconciler *reads and merges* — so a folder you declared before granting it, or a service you named
  before connecting it, stays put.
- **They heal.** The moment the real thing appears — you connect that service, grant that folder — the
  live version takes over the same card (declared → ready), with no duplicate. Remove and undo work on
  declarations exactly as on everything else, and **re-declaring something you removed brings it back**
  (an explicit "put it on the table" overrides a prior removal).
- **Set one up now** — the palette also links straight into the existing connect / credential / forge
  flows. Declaring never writes those stores or configures anything; it's a note to yourself, and setup
  still happens where it always did (no dual-write).

## Secrets & safety

A credential you put on the table is a **name only** — never a value, and a declared credential carries
no free-text note at all, so a secret can't be parked on a Keys-zone card even by accident. Declarations
are operator-typed, so they're trusted input, and their origin can never be laundered into or out of
that trust. The new declaration store is UI-owned and read-only to the reconciler, so the projection
snapshot keeps its single writer — declarations can't race the reconcile.

An adversarial review (5 lenses × refute-by-default panels) cleared the taint/provenance, no-secret,
no-dual-write, and merge/heal invariants; the one real finding it surfaced (a removed item's
re-declaration being swallowed by a stale tombstone while the UI claimed success) is fixed here. Full
suite: 5676+ passed.

## Scope

This is the *declare* half. The richer **"+ Put on the table"** entries — searching the MCP registry and
forging a tool with pre-filled context, verify-now, and one-click repair on broken cards — are the next
slice (T2b-2).
