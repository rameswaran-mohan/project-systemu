# v0.10.19 — Measuring asks that skipped self-service (R-A13.5, avoidable-ask)

Completing the measurement pair from v0.10.18: alongside redundant tool-forging, the system now records
and reports how often it asked you something without first trying to resolve it itself.

## What ships

- **An ask corpus.** At the point the system decides to ask the harness for a tool, credential, or
  decision, it records the ask along with what it had already tried (resolution attempts, blocking
  signals) — an append-only, observability-only log that never affects the run that made the ask.
- **`sharing-on debug avoidable-ask`** — reports the fraction of asks made with *no recorded resolution
  attempt* (nothing tried, nothing blocking). By the "ask freely, but try inventory/discovery/resolver
  first" policy, those are the avoidable-ask candidates. It's a deterministic replay — no AI judgment —
  reported beside the avoidable-forge rate.

## Honest about what it measures

This is a deterministic **lower bound**, not the definitive avoidable-ask rate: it counts asks that
skipped self-service outright. The definitive rate — "*could* the inventory/resolver have answered
this?" — needs replaying the resolver against the situation snapshot the ask was made in, which is the
documented next refinement. Operator-input asks (where the system genuinely needs your input) are
excluded, since those are necessary by nature.

Together the avoidable-forge and avoidable-ask rates are the measurement substrate the roadmap has been
accreting toward — the data that will decide the quick-lane ask policy and validate the embedding-free
tool selection.

An adversarial review checked that the recorder can never affect the shadow run, records only
non-secret signals, excludes operator-input asks, and labels the metric honestly. Full suite green.
